# 30/60/90-Day Ramp Plan — Template

A reusable ramp plan that gives a new hire and their manager a shared, explicit picture of what success looks like in the first three months. Fill it in per hire and adapt the depth to the role and level: an IC ramps into execution; a manager/leader ramps into people and strategy; a junior hire ramps with more learning weight up front. Keep goals few, concrete, and observable. Store the completed plan in the hire's `onboarding/<employee-slug>/` folder and share it with the hire.

> This is a **draft plan for the manager to own and adjust** — not a performance guarantee or a legal document. Any probationary/introductory-period terms are policy- and jurisdiction-specific — verify separately.

## The three windows

Each window balances four lanes. Keep the learning-heavy weight early and shift toward independent delivery by day 90.

- **Learn** — what to understand (product/business, team, tools, customers, process)
- **Do** — concrete deliverables and contributions
- **Connect** — relationships to build (team, cross-functional, stakeholders)
- **Milestones** — observable checkpoints that show the ramp is on track

### First 30 days — Learn & land
| Lane | Goals |
|---|---|
| Learn | Company/business context (from ABOUT.md), team norms, core tools, key policies (handbook), the role's success metrics |
| Do | Complete onboarding tasks; ship 1–2 small, low-risk early wins |
| Connect | Manager 1:1 cadence set; buddy relationship active; meet immediate team |
| Milestones | Access working; required/compliance training underway; can navigate day-to-day independently |
| Check-in | **30-day review** (manager + HR): experience, blockers, goals on track |

### 31–60 days — Contribute
| Lane | Goals |
|---|---|
| Learn | Deeper domain/role knowledge; edge cases; how work flows across teams |
| Do | Own a meaningful piece of work end-to-end with support |
| Connect | Build key cross-functional relationships; contribute in team forums |
| Milestones | Delivering with lighter supervision; role-specific training progressing |
| Check-in | **60-day review**: ramping pace, two-way feedback, adjust the plan |

### 61–90 days — Own
| Lane | Goals |
|---|---|
| Learn | Fill remaining gaps; understand longer-term priorities |
| Do | Own core responsibilities at expected quality and pace |
| Connect | Trusted, contributing team member; proactive collaborator |
| Milestones | Operating independently; meeting the "fully ramped" definition above |
| Check-in | **90-day review**: fully-ramped assessment vs. plan; transition to the regular performance cycle |
