# Job Description Template

A reusable, inclusive job-description structure. Fill every `<placeholder>`; delete guidance in _italics_. Ground the company summary in `company.about_file` and the tone in `company.soul_file`. Do not invent company facts, benefits, or comp numbers.

---

## Structure

### Job title
`<Clear, standard, searchable title>` — _use the title candidates would search for, not an internal or inflated one ("Senior Accountant", not "Finance Ninja"). Avoid gendered or age-coded terms._

### Location & work model
`<City, Region>` · `<On-site | Hybrid (X days) | Remote (eligible locations)>` — _state the real expectation; align to `company.remote_policy` and the role's jurisdiction._

### About `<Company>`
_2–4 sentences from `company.about_file`: what the company does, who it serves, why the work matters. Neutral, honest, brand-voiced._

### The role — summary
_2–3 sentences: the mission of the role, the team and reporting line, the outcome it owns. Lead with impact, not a list._

### What you'll do (responsibilities)
- `<Outcome-oriented responsibility>`
- `<...>`
_5–8 bullets. Describe outcomes and the actual work, not an exhaustive task dump. Start with verbs._

### Required qualifications (must-haves)
- `<Genuinely essential, disqualifying criterion>`
_Keep this list short — every item excludes candidates. Prefer demonstrable skills/competencies over years-of-experience proxies or specific degrees unless truly required. Avoid "X+ years" as a stand-in for skill where you can name the skill instead._

### Preferred qualifications (nice-to-haves)
- `<Helpful but not disqualifying>`
_Explicitly labeled "preferred" so candidates who lack them still apply. Research shows some candidates only apply when they meet ~all listed criteria; a bloated required list shrinks and skews the pool._

### Compensation
`<Pay range: $X–$Y per <period>>` — _pull the band from the hr-compensation-benefits skill / `compensation-benefits/` folder; never invent it._ Add benefits highlights only from stated company facts.

> ⚠️ **Pay-range-in-posting is legally required in some jurisdictions** (pay-transparency laws) and optional in others. Whether the range must appear here — and in what form — depends on the role's location as resolved against `legal.jurisdictions`. Confirm the requirement and verify with counsel before posting. If `legal.jurisdictions` is empty, treat this as unknown and flag it.

### How to apply
_Short, accessible process. Minimal required fields. State realistic timeline and what happens next — good candidate experience starts here._

### Equal-opportunity / EEO statement
`<EEO statement>` — _placeholder. The exact required wording, protected categories, and any accommodation/e-verify language are jurisdiction-specific; source approved wording from the client and verify with counsel. Do not fabricate statutory language._

## Inclusive-language do / don't checklist

Run this against every JD before posting.

**Do**
- Use gender-neutral language ("they", "the candidate"); check tools like a bias/gender-decoder mindset for skewed wording.
- Describe outcomes and competencies; name the skill instead of a years-of-experience proxy where possible.
- Keep required qualifications minimal and truly essential; move the rest to "preferred".
- Write in plain language; expand or avoid internal jargon and acronyms.
- State location, work model, and (where required/chosen) pay range transparently.
- Emphasize what the person will achieve and learn, not just demands.

**Don't**
- Use gender-coded words ("rockstar", "ninja", "aggressive", "dominant", "nurturing", "he/she").
- Use age-coded terms ("young", "digital native", "recent grad", "energetic") or culture-fit language that codes for sameness.
- Require a specific degree, school, or "X+ years" when a demonstrable skill would do.
- List every conceivable skill as "required" — it narrows and biases the pool.
- Imply physical or other requirements unrelated to the job's essential functions.
- Copy statutory or EEO wording from memory — use the client's approved, counsel-verified text.

## Reminders

- The JD is a draft for HR/hiring-manager review, not legal advice.
- Comp range = compensation's number, flagged jurisdiction-specific for posting.
- Save the drafted JD to `recruitment/` (or `recruitment/<role-slug>/`).
